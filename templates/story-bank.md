# Story Bank

Your personal library of STAR-format behavioral stories.
Fill in each story with your real experience. Practice saying them out loud.
The goal is not to memorize — the goal is to internalize so the story flows naturally.

---

## How to Use This

1. Read each story prompt
2. Write your real story in the STAR format below each one
3. Practice saying it out loud — aim for 2 minutes max
4. After each practice, ask: Was it specific? Did I say "I" not just "we"? Did I give a real outcome?

---

## Story Template Format

```
SITUATION: [What was the context? What was at stake? 2-3 sentences.]

TASK: [What was your specific responsibility? 1-2 sentences.]

ACTION: [What did YOU specifically do? Step by step. 3-5 sentences. This is the most important part.]

RESULT: [What was the outcome? Quantify if possible. What did you learn? 2-3 sentences.]
```

## Story Retrieval Drills

Practice pulling the right story for a question quickly. Under pressure, candidates forget their best stories and tell weak ones instead.

| Interviewer Asks | Best Story to Tell |
|---|---|
| "Tell me about a failure" | Story 1 (bug in production) |
| "Tell me about handling pressure" | Story 2 (pushing back on release) |
| "Tell me about a conflict" | Story 3 (developer dispute) |
| "Tell me about process improvement" | Story 4 (QA culture improvement) |
| "Tell me about your automation work" | Story 5 (framework) |
| "Tell me about communicating bad news" | Story 6 |
| "Tell me about learning fast" | Story 7 |
| "Tell me about ambiguity" | Story 8 |
| "Tell me about leadership or mentorship" | Story 9 |
| "Tell me about a mistake" | Story 10 |
| "Tell me about your proudest accomplishment" | Story 12 |

## Story Quality Checklist

Before every interview, review your stories against this checklist:

- [ ] Every story uses "I" not just "we"
- [ ] Every story has a specific, concrete outcome
- [ ] At least 3 stories have quantified results (%, time saved, bugs prevented, etc.)
- [ ] No story is longer than 2.5 minutes when spoken aloud
- [ ] Every failure story ends with what changed as a result
- [ ] You can tell every story without looking at notes
- [ ] Stories span multiple companies or projects (diversity of experience)
- [ ] At least one story involves cross-functional collaboration (not just within QA)
