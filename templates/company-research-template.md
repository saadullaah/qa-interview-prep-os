# Company Research Template

Fill this out before every interview loop. Reference it in your answers.
Candidates who mention company-specific details in interviews stand out immediately.

---

## Company: [Name]
## Role: [Title]
## Interview Date: [Date]
## Interview Loop Stage: [Recruiter / Technical / Hiring Manager / Final]

## Section 1 — The Basics

**What does the company do?**
(2-3 sentences, in plain English. Can you explain it to a non-technical friend?)


**Who are their main customers?**
(B2B? B2C? Enterprise? SMB? Consumer?)


**What is their business model?**
(SaaS subscription? Marketplace? Advertising? E-commerce?)


**How big is the company?**
(Employees, revenue if known, funding stage)


**Where are they in their growth stage?**
(Early startup / Series A-C / Late stage / Public)


**Who are their main competitors?**


**What makes them different from competitors?**

## Section 2 — The Product

**What is the core product?**


**What platforms does it run on?**
(Web? iOS? Android? Desktop? API-only? Embedded?)


**How complex is the product from a QA perspective?**
(Simple CRUD app? Complex financial transactions? Real-time data? Integrations?)


**What are the most critical user flows from a quality standpoint?**
(Payment? Onboarding? Core feature? Search?)


**What are the most likely quality risk areas?**
(Based on product type — data integrity, performance, security, cross-device, etc.)


**Have they had any public quality issues?**
(Production outages, security breaches, data loss incidents — check TechCrunch, Twitter/X, Hacker News, status pages)

## Section 7 — Company-Specific Talking Points

Write 2-3 specific things you can drop naturally into the interview to signal research:

1. "I noticed from your engineering blog that your team [X] — I have experience with exactly that situation at [Y]..."
2. "I saw you recently [launched / raised / announced] [X] — I imagine that creates interesting quality challenges around [Y]..."
3. "From what I understand about your product, the most critical quality risks would be [X] — is that consistent with what the team is focused on?"
